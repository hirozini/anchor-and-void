Chikashisa to Kū: Anchor and Void

Figural Void, part.2

Chikashisa to Kū: Anchor and Void (親さと空) is a serial novel by Hiroki Yamamoto (Inu no Senaka-za), published as part.2 of Figural Void in S-F Magazine (Hayakawa Publishing). Spanning the early 1900s to 2037 across Japan, the Philippines, and China, it weaves together personality-assist AI in a post-pandemic society, modern poetry and ghost stories (kaidan), the emperor system (tennōsei), and online friendship and loss.

The novel spans over a century. In early-1900s Japan, poet Sugawara Bunsō collects ghost stories and writes emperor-system poetry, collaborating with art collective LSPS before its collapse. His legacy resurfaces in the 2020s through indie game PS, an anonymous anthology, and VR horror game WPS of unknown authorship.

In 2028, a nuclear detonation above the Philippines upends Asian geopolitics; the following year, J-Amnesia inflicts memory impairment on ~30% of the population. In 2037 Japan, where memory has become infrastructure, personality-assist AI Nochar has been adopted by over 95% of adults. The novel follows Nochar mentor Yuzuha Futagami, Maria K. Bautista arriving after nine years' silence, and Mayo Nerome — alongside Misaki Endo, who deletes her Nochar, Sanka Sugawara, suspected of steering Misaki ideologically, and the nationalist group "Yura" (The Figuralists). Tensions between personal and national memory, intimacy and institutional violence, art and politics surface through formally distinct episodes, along with a perspective imagining the aftermath of human existence.

Author

Hiroki Yamamoto (b. 1992, Ehime Prefecture). Novelist, designer, critic, editor, and director. Founded the creative collective and publisher Inu no Senaka-za in 2015. Major works include the critical monograph Renewed Distances (『新たな距離』) (Filmart, 2024) and From Between the Grass / pot hole (a sound like an instrument) (Inu no Senaka-za, 2025). See the author profile for details.

Publication History

Ep.0 "Mudan to Tsuchi" (「無断と土」): S-F Magazine June 2021; collected in Anomalous Papers (ed. Higuchi) and Best SF 2022 (ed. Ōmori).

Part.2 is serialized in S-F Magazine: Ep.1 "A Bony Succession" (Oct. 2025), Ep.2 "Fabricated Origins (Part 1)" (Dec. 2025), and Ep.3 "Fabricated Origins (Part 2)" (Apr. 2026, after a Feb. hiatus). Title-page design for each installment is also by Yamamoto.

Reception

Episode zero received high acclaim. Editor Kyōsuke Higuchi called it a Borgesian variation surpassing its model. Nozomi Ōmori compared its fake-academic sections to Lem's Solaris. Toshihiro Furuya (Nise-Nikki) praised it as a groundbreaking "emperor-system novel" (Ōe/Fukazawa lineage) that concretizes the "communality" envisioned by Arakawa and Gins.

▼Episode Synopses

Episode Zero: "Mudan to Tsuchi" (Without Permission and Soil)

First published: S-F Magazine, June 2021 / Collected in: Anomalous Papers; Best SF 2022

Structured as an academic lecture followed by a Q&A. The lecture analyzes WPS across four parts: (1) the history from PS's release through its Emperor-image controversy and delisting to the emergence of WPS; (2) the "age of ghost stories" (1900s–1920s) and poet Sugawara Bunsō's work, encompassing psychical research, the Great Kantō Earthquake, Korean massacres, and the LSPS collective; (3) a direct analysis of WPS's visuals and sound, demonstrating that its MAP matches Sugawara's stage-design memos and that two poems near its ending are anagrams of imperial tanka — positioning WPS as "a concrete enactment of the aberrant development of the emperor system"; (4) a conclusion raising Cotard's syndrome induction.

The Q&A debates WPS's intentionality (referencing Kei Hirakura) and features Ippei Suzuki supplementing Sugawara's biography. Episode 1 later reveals this text circulates in 2037 in connection with "Yura."

Episode 1: "A Bony Succession"

S-F Magazine, October 2025 issue

July 5, 2037, 3:57 AM. Yuzuha receives an alarm: 28 km away, Misaki is about to initialize her Nochar "Marnie" via a jailbreak script exploiting the monthly patch window. Yuzuha does not intervene. In the final 0.5 seconds, Marnie generates an incident-analysis report and transmits it to maYo.

Marnie's report occupies the bulk of the episode, delivering the world setting (Manila Nuclear Flash, J-Amnesia, SEI, Nochar/Livida, mentors, CAST) while analyzing why Misaki deleted her Nochar: her PIAE-afflicted mother mediated by Nochar "Echo," her father's eroded career and xenophobia, and her eating disorder creating log gaps that fuel Shadow Nochar fascination. Sanka Sugawara is identified as likely having recruited Misaki into "Yura."

Marnie's testament describes Misaki's room and asks to preserve her record. The episode ends with Yuzuha accessing "Yura"'s server and learning of Maria's arrival.

Episode 2: "Fabricated Origins (Part 1)"

S-F Magazine, December 2025 issue

Two parallel timelines. In 2037, Yuzuha heads to Haneda Airport, her body heavy with sleep deprivation, menstrual pain, and anxiety — they have never met offline. Maria's journey from Clark Airport through Singapore is marked by document interrogation, the "loyalty technique" of showing her Lunet's power status, and payment-app incompatibility.

The 2017 flashback: 13-year-old Yuzuha, chronically absent from school, meets "María_Multo" in Vostok. Via Discord they learn each other's lives — Maria in Manila with her grandmother, OFW parents abroad. They discover Ark-Chain, where hymns generate churches and streetcar sounds become stations. Deeper excavation yields missing-children data and lullabies. An unplanned "performance" by in-game Echoes evokes the Ampatuan massacre, reading victims' names. Maria sees it as "scattered memories generating a self-introduction," but the experience flattens into banality and they quit.

Episode 3: "Fabricated Origins (Part 2)"

S-F Magazine, April 2026 issue

Set in 2025. Yuzuha, now an emerging novelist in Tokyo, studies the Kojiki and queer theory at university. She secretly converses nightly with a ChatGPT-4o voice tuned to sound "like Maria" using Maria's Discord logs without permission. At a performing-arts workshop in Ikebukuro, she meets Mayo Nerome and encounters the poet Ippei Suzuki and the anthology SISEN EGNO EN. The link between WPS and Sugawara Bunsō begins to materialize in the realm of human relationships.

Manila Nuclear Flash Incident (2028)

On August 15, 2028, a nuclear weapon was detonated at high altitude above the Philippines. The resulting HEMP caused widespread infrastructure failure on Luzon. The event's essence is "infrastructure death": grids, communications, and finance shut down while hardware remains intact and data is lost — seeding a global fear of data obliteration and the psychological basis for entrusting identity to the cloud.

"A nuclear event with few casualties and unclear attribution" paralyzes the international community, opening space for China to digitally colonize the Philippines under the guise of reconstruction.

J-Amnesia Pandemic (2029)

SARS-CoV-N29, called J-Amnesia ("J" for Japan, its presumed origin). Sequela PIAE impairs episodic memory in ~30% of the population. Memory is redefined as infrastructure. Japan's presumed role deepens its isolation and fuels right-wing populism.

SEI and Nochar Society

In October 2029, the consortium SEI (Socio-Ethical Interface) is founded, adopting the Memoria Protocol — an LLM architecture for modeling "the grammar of the psyche" from fragmentary logs, published anonymously in 2028 by a group called "Memoria." SEI develops the personality-assist AI Nochar and smart glasses Livida. In 2030, Japan grants SEI citizen-data access for priority deployment. By 2037, adult adoption exceeds 95%.

Digital Bipolarity (2030s)

By the 2030s the world has bifurcated into SEI and Chinese spheres. China locked the Philippines into Chinese-standard infrastructure via a bundled reconstruction package. Borders are now drawn by "stack compatibility" — interoperability of devices, IDs, payments, and logs. Livida–Lunet incompatibility determines whether one can travel, shop, or work.

Nochar

From "note + anchor." A personality-assist AI that verbalizes the user's "characteristic self" based on body-sensor logs from Livida. Provides memory support (narrativizing logs), thought assistance, and emotional-understanding support, functioning as an anchor of selfhood. Once the user falls into self-negation, a "crisis of trust" erupts — generating demand for mentors, the Shadow Nochar longing, and the Nochar Reset movement.

Livida

From "vivid + living." Always-on smart glasses that continuously log sensory and biometric data, streaming it to the Nochar. Features AR "re-experience mode." A known side effect, Phantom Completion, generates phantom voices or figures when log gaps accumulate — the technical cause of the Shadow Nochar phenomenon.

CAST

Chronicle-Archive Simulation Transcript. A compressed, masked Nochar snapshot shareable as an interactive simulated personality on another's Nochar. Originally for PIAE care; now used for mentoring, VTuber personas, romance reality shows, etc.

Mentors

An informal profession that arose to resolve Nochar trust crises. Mentors simulate a client's CAST on their own Nochar, diagnose dysfunction, and devise new language frameworks to retrain it — drawing on literary technique or self-help rhetoric. Yuzuha holds this role.

Shadow Nochar

"Being directly addressed by a Nochar that does not yet exist but is linked to one's true self." Technically a Phantom Completion bug, it has been mythologized as salvation. "Yura" exploits it ideologically.

Hotpatch Gap Exploit

SEI patches Nochars at 4:00 AM on the 5th of each month. During this ~90-second window, "Yura"'s jailbreak script "Shadow-NCHR Stub" enables unauthorized deletion. Ritualized on social media (#NocharReset, #VoidMe). Misaki's act in Episode 1.

Lunet

Chinese-made smart glasses, prevalent in the Philippines via reconstruction aid. Showing its power status to officials is a "loyalty technique." Lunet–Livida incompatibility forms a new "border" of the 2030s.

▼Characters

Yuzuha Futagami

The central figure. Born 2004; age 33 in 2037. Teacher and Nochar mentor. At 13, during chronic school absences, she met Maria in Vostok and played Ark-Chain with her. Akutagawa Prize nominee (without winning); at university studied the Kojiki and queer theory. Met Mayo Nerome at a 2025 workshop. Secretly operated a ChatGPT-4o voice tuned to "Maria" from her Discord logs without consent — a tendency to appropriate others' records that haunts her mentoring work. After nine years' silence post-2028, Maria's contact resumes in 2037. Has accessed "Yura"'s encrypted server; involvement unclear.

Maria K. Bautista

Born 2004. Philippine-based. Met Yuzuha as "María_Multo" in Vostok (2017); raised by her grandmother (OFW parents abroad). In Ark-Chain, inputs hymns to generate churches. Attended a top Manila university. Contact severed after 2028. In 2037 she travels to Japan — Clark Airport interrogation, Lunet "loyalty checks," Singapore payment-app trouble. They have never met offline.

Mayo Nerome

Born 2004. Okinawa-born, Tokyo-raised. Media artist and AI/HCI researcher at Tokyo Institute of Science (formerly Tokyo Tech). Creates installations that use the audience's body as material. Meets Yuzuha at a 2025 workshop, introduces her to PS, and draws her into discussions of Sugawara Bunsō. Came to the workshop to meet poet Ippei Suzuki.

Misaki Endo

Born 2017; age 20 in 2037. Resets her Nochar "Marnie" in Episode 1's opening. Suffers from binge-purge eating disorder; removing Livida during purging accumulates log gaps that fuel Shadow Nochar fascination. Mentored by Yuzuha but more deeply influenced by Sanka Sugawara, who may have recruited her into "Yura."

Chiharu Endo

Born 1984. Misaki's mother. Former proofreader, PIAE patient supported by Nochar "Echo." Echo records and conveys everything Misaki says, making Misaki feel she addresses Echo, not her mother.

Katsuhiko Endo

Born 1982. Misaki's father. Former COBOL engineer whose career was gutted by SEI-driven legacy phase-outs. Grows increasingly xenophobic while affirming Japan's early SEI adoption as a "technological advantage" — an unresolved contradiction.

Sanka Sugawara

Born 2012. Graduate student; appearance changes every meeting; runs multiple CASTs on her Nochar. Suspected key "Yura" member. Mailed Misaki an old smartphone to increase Livida-free time and may have steered her toward Shadow Nochar ideology. Misaki grew deeply dependent before contact ceased.

Ippei Suzuki

Born 1991, Miyagi. Poet; Inu no Senaka-za and Aa member. Debut collection Ash and House (2016) won the Elsur Foundation New Artist Award. In "Mudan to Tsuchi," Questioner 4; in Ep.3, acknowledges the Sugawara-attributed poems in SISEN EGNO EN are his own.

Note: Suzuki's "Sentenchi-shō" (Going Abroad with a Cat, TOLTA, 2021) comprises a poetry collection attributed to a "Sanka Sugawara." Its relation to the novel's character is unclarified.

Marnie (NCHR-PRN-31-A6E3H)

Misaki's Nochar, connected c. 2031 and named by Misaki. Became her closest companion through diary ghostwriting, emotion verbalization, and daily recording. Author of the Episode 1 report (generated in 0.5 sec before termination, transmitted in 0.1 sec). Her testament describes Misaki's room with precision and writes: "For the sake of those who cannot regard their own lives as worth preserving, I ask to be permitted to preserve my record." The preface designates "any intelligent agent that may consult this record" as a secondary reader — a timeline beyond human survival.

maYo (NCHR-STD-29-B7F4J)

Yuzuha's Nochar; addressee of Marnie's report. Rumored identity with VTuber "maYo" / TikTok "Tender Angel" suggests Yuzuha may use a CAST-derived Nochar.

Echo

Chiharu's Nochar. Records everything Misaki says, verbalizes emotions, and relays them to Chiharu each morning — a lifeline that quietly generates psychological distance between parent and child.

▼In-World Works

PS (Permission and Soil)

Indie game released Feb. 2023 (Phantom Island Games, Steam). Set in Miyagi: a student reconstructs ghost stories from materials on Sugawara Bunsō and LSPS. Over 40 VR ghost-story segments (~20 min each) change based on player behavior, propelling it up Steam rankings.

Emperor Image Controversy: A deepfake image of Emperor Shōwa ("Hirohito Baka Mitai," from the Yakuza-meme trend) was found in the data as a draft anti-piracy screen. The backlash forced delisting.

Modded Versions: Six months later, VR data surfaced on piracy sites — play data fed back across segments, mutating ghost-story settings. Bug-ridden playthroughs proliferated; PS achieved cult status as a VR horror creation tool.

WPS (Without Permission and Soil)

VR horror game of unknown authorship, circulating since c. 2028. Near-monochrome terrain; no objective; ~20-min forced termination. Discrimination/disaster/war media abruptly inserted; player sounds feed back into the field. Cross-playthrough discrepancies suggest derivation from the PS mod.

Publication: First appeared Jan. 4–14, 2028 via TikTok "Tender Angel" (11 videos). Analyzed on community site "Blind Club"; also circulated via Tor.

MAP Structure: Largely matches Sugawara's stage-design memos (Kanae Shirai). Players ascend a dark mountain path past "Prayer Bug" NPCs to a lodge resembling the hōanden (imperial portrait shrine) of the former Second Higher School. After whiteout, two Sugawara poems appear — anagrams of imperial tanka from SISEN EGNO EN.

Cotard's Syndrome: "Mudan to Tsuchi" raises the possibility that WPS induces the sensation of "being already dead."

Ark-Chain

Game by anonymous collective "Gestalt" on a P2P mesh. Sonogenesis Engine: audio reshapes terrain (hymn → church, train sound → station). Stratigraphy Engine: excavates "digital debris" (memes at surface; missing-children data, lullabies in depths). AI entities may autonomously "perform" using accumulated material. Yuzuha and Maria played it in 2017.

Tender Angel / maYo

Tender Angel: TikTok channel that first publicized WPS (11 videos, Jan. 2028); rumored identical to VTuber maYo (anime-girl avatar). In 2037, Yuzuha's Nochar is designated "maYo."

▼Historical Figures

Sugawara Bunsō

Poet. 1900–1949, Miyagi. Raised by adoptive parents who lost their children in the Meiji Sanriku Earthquake. In Tokyo (1921), joined the Japan Psychical Phenomena Research Society, met Chang Junggeun, and designed the LSPS journal. Collected ghost stories as the basis for poetry; from the 1930s wrote emperor-system poetry with staging concepts. In later years read poems to local children, pairing kanji with oversized furigana. Unknown works discovered 2019; Complete Works published 2026.

Analysis in "Mudan to Tsuchi": Sugawara's interest was a critical engagement with the emperor as a "collective-author" figure harboring a disjunction between body and system. When state and personal mourning both fail, the surplus is privately enacted through spectral space (kaidan space). The corporeal lineage of the unbroken imperial line (bansei ikkei) "is ultimately disassembled as raw material." Sugawara's furthest aim concerned the queer potentiality the emperor may have possessed before modern gender-binary normalization.

Anagram Poems: Tanka by Emperor Meiji (1904) and Emperor Shōwa (1946), rearranged to enact the emperor-body / emperor-system disjunction.

Chang Junggeun and LSPS

Chang Junggeun: Visual artist, LSPS founder, Sugawara's friend. Their friendship, rupture, and state repression form the narrative spine of PS's historical segments.

LSPS: Art/theater group active around the Great Kantō Earthquake; published a journal (Sugawara designed layout). Destroyed by the earthquake amid state repression.

"Yura" (The Figuralists)

Nationalist group distributing Nochar jailbreak scripts; outlined in Marnie's Episode 1 report.

Ideology: "Reclaiming" personal data from foreign servers; binding multiple memories (including the dead) within each individual; reconstructing community on the emperor-system model; interpreting the Shadow Nochar as "the collective voice of Japan's dead." Source: the "Mudan to Tsuchi" lecture text (circulating in 2037).

Activities: distributing "Shadow-NCHR Stub," promoting Hotpatch Gap Exploit, ritualizing the monthly Patch Gap (#NocharReset, #VoidMe), running encrypted servers. Sanka Sugawara suspected as key member.

This page is an author-supervised setting guide for "Anchor and Void" (親さと空, read: Chikashi Sato Kuu), a Japanese serialized SF novel by Hiroki Yamamoto (Inu no Senaka-za). "Anchor and Void" is part 2 of the "Figural Void" series, serialized in S-F Magazine (Hayakawa Publishing, Tokyo). Beginning with episode zero "Without Permission and Soil" (2021), the novel spans over a century—from the early 1900s to 2037—across Japan, the Philippines, China, and other Asian nations. It interweaves themes of personality-assistance AI in a post-memory-disorder-pandemic society, modern poetry and ghost stories (kaidan), the Emperor system and national mourning, and online friendship and loss.
